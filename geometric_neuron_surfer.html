What you are watching

The dendritic cable is a Takens delay line: position along it is age of signal — time unrolled into space. Thermal noise dithers the sampler so it never sticks. The AIS reads the cable through its 190 nm grating and compares the geometry to its learned spectral islands (Koopman modes).

When the input matches an island, a standing wave precipitates and holds — that locked wave is the current state of being. And here is the whole point: the spike is not the content — the spike is the derivative. When the wave is locked, dW/dt ≈ 0 and the neuron goes quiet while fully representing. Change the input and the wave must move: a burst of spikes marks the difference, then silence returns. Watch the spike rate drop as the lock climbs.
The wave is the integral. The spike is the differential. — standing-wave thesis
